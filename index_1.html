The story behind Kanasu

Engineer.
Artist. Healer.

I was born in India and came to art in my early twenties — not as a career plan, but as a way to process and accept what life had handed me. Art gave me a language for what I couldn't otherwise express, and it changed everything.

I began with acrylic painting and pouring, then fell into polymer clay jewelry. I established Kanasu with a clear purpose: to create beautiful things and donate proceeds to local women's shelters — because I believe deeply in the power of support, and in reaching women who still need it. Kanasu was selected to showcase at art fairs across San Diego before a growing family brought that chapter to a pause.

Postpartum, ceramics found me. What began as a few hours of quiet studio time became a lifeline back to myself. If I could draw one throughline through my life, it would be art. It has met me at every difficult crossing and carried me forward.

"Art shouldn't have a high barrier to entry — it is too powerful, too therapeutic, too human for that. My goal is to give back some of the peace I have found."
